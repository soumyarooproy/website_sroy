Soumyaroop Roy

Fast is better than slow - I like fast code

I am a computer engineer and programmer. My technical areas of interest include web-scalability and distributed systems, high-performance compute kernels, and computer architecture and compilers. I have a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from University of South Florida in Tampa, FL where I worked on low-power architectures and compilers. I have served as a reviewer and technical program committee member in several conferences and journals on computer architecture. In late 2017, I started studying the field of deep learning, primarily from a high-performance and distributed computing perspective.

Most recently, while working at Bloomberg in San Francisco in the back-end infrastructure team for Bquant Platform, I developed and deployed a highly-available SaaS application for serving Conda packages to the Bquant installer. Before that, I worked at Advanced Micro Devices where I managed the performance verification project -- ensuring that the performance of the CPU core was as spec'ed -- for the Zen core and also owned the floating-point unit performance model.

I live in the San Francisco bay area with my lovely wife Denise and our dog, Turing.
